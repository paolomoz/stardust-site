01 ·The shift Web publishing changed under us

The library was
a workaround.

Assemble pages from shared parts.
→
Generate unique pages that share the same rules.
The old deal

Reuse everything. Build nothing new.

Implementation partners shipped a library — Hero, Card Grid, Accordion, Tabs. Marketers composed pages from the shelf. Sites got fast by getting boring. It worked.

What broke it

The cost of uniqueness collapsed.

AI agents now generate bespoke, production-quality HTML — scroll-driven motion, cinematic transitions, three-week designs in an hour — from a prompt and a brand guide.

Now

Every page can be its own page again.

Provided the brand keeps the rules. The library's job moves up a level — from shape to guardrails.

02 ·What brands gain Expressiveness · without losing the brand

Apple-grade craft —
on every page.

The Apple iPad page. The Spotify campaign that moves like a film. The Nike launch that feels nothing like a template. Six-figure projects with months of lead time — that ceiling is gone.

Expressiveness at scale

Every campaign carries the craft.

Not just hero activations that survive the annual budget. Every market launch, every product moment, every micro-site — distinct, on-brand, deserving of attention.

No handover

The seam is gone.

Designers work on the live page. Marketing edits in-context. Translations run in parallel. SEO tunes the real thing. Nothing waits on anything.

Control moves up a level

Tokens, not templates.

Type, color, spacing, assets — enforced at the platform. AI review at publish: brand, a11y, perf, legal. Markets get latitude; the brand keeps the non-negotiables.

03 ·Strategy Sell modernization · not migration

The refresh
is the pitch.

Brands tire of their visual identity every 12–24 months. The impulse is real — but expensive enough that most settle for staying static between major rebrands. Bundling the refresh with a platform migration changes the math: one project, two outcomes — a modern stack and a modern identity.

Performance, SEO, and LLM visibility ride along by default.

Stardust

Generation, with the brand pinned.

Crawls the live site, diagnoses pitfalls, proposes fresh directions, regenerates every page under a new design system. Palette, typography, voice — pinned, so identity survives.

Snowflake

Any AI page → editable EDS site.

Drop AI prototypes into a known folder; out comes a publishable EDS site with the authoring stack already wired: in-page editing, scheduling, drafts, edge routing.

Together

Pixel-perfect, by construction.

Stardust outputs clean, semantic markup. Snowflake maps it onto Edge Delivery's blocks without a hand-off step. The migration to EDS isn't a step — it's the same artifact, reframed.
