Tensions

Mechanical detector output · 5 tensions surfaced. Each is a candidate weakness for the redesign brief — they feed stardust/prototypes/home-improvements.md.

P1 · Contrast / hero

Hero h1 sits on photography without a contrast scrim

The h1 (#0578ff) renders over a photographic background of an arm + phone. Across the photograph the contrast varies; large portions of the headline reach the photo's mid-tone band where AA contrast is borderline. There is no scrim or backdrop blur. Fix candidate: add a low-opacity gradient scrim or move the headline onto a flat alt-section background.

P1 · IA / redundancy

Five separate "Sign up / Get Started / Join now" CTAs

The conversion path is repeated in five distinct bands (header, hero, mid-page callout, employer-strip, footer-callout). The repetition is intentional but reads as anxious — the same offer appears too many times without earning it through new context. Fix candidate: consolidate to 3 strategic reprises with distinct framings (try, buy, gift / employer / member).

P2 · System / tokens

Zero CSS custom properties at :root

The site ships no design tokens — colors and spacing are baked into stylesheet rules. This forces every section to redeclare values; small palette changes require sweeping CSS edits. Fix candidate: introduce a token contract (the redesign will produce a :root block per stardust's token-contract.md).

P2 · Type / scale

Display sizes 32 / 48 / 72 / 88 don't form a modular ratio

The site uses an ad-hoc type scale — ratios 1.5 / 1.83 / 2.25 between display sizes mean the headline rhythm is uneven. The 88px rotating-services h2 dwarfs the 72px section h2s by a barely-distinguishable margin, and the 48px callout sits awkwardly between 32 and 72. Fix candidate: tighten to a 1.25 or 1.333 ratio with no more than 4 display sizes.

P3 · Photography / sizing

The 3-col preview cards crop the photographs to thumbnail-size

OneMedical owns excellent photography (the Meditation, Exam Room, and Pharmacy shots are warm and editorial), but the 3-col grid renders each photo at ~33% width with limited vertical real estate. The product is reduced where its strongest brand asset could lead. Fix candidate: let one card go full-width / full-bleed on at least one breakpoint, or upgrade the 3-col card to use 5/3 split (photo dominant).

P3 · Header / accessibility

Header is absolute-positioned over a photographic hero

The white header sits over the photographic hero; nav buttons inherit dark-jade text on light backgrounds when scrolled, but the initial state has a partial-transparency band where focus order and contrast are both borderline. Fix candidate: commit to a solid header or add a contrast-aware ground (frosted band or contrast-aware text).
