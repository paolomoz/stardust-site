04 · Travel & access

Travel & access

The expansion of Heathrow is a unique opportunity to change the way that people and goods travel to, from and around the airport. Improving surface access brings benefits to passengers, colleagues, and to local communities around the airport.

We have pledged to deliver expansion with no more airport-related traffic on the roads than there is today. The Airports NPS sets targets to increase public transport mode share to at least 50% of passenger journeys by 2030 and 55% by 2040, and to reduce colleague car trips by 25% by 2030 and 50% by 2040 from a 2013 baseline.

Seven proposals, one strategy

Our Surface Access Proposals document covers seven key areas — public transport, colleague travel, car parking, road user charging, taxi and private hire, freight, and intelligent mobility. Each builds on the others to deliver our sustainability commitments and meet the Airports NPS targets.

6.1

Public transport

Our proposals are based on three principles: making best use of existing public transport, supporting committed improvements, and developing new public transport routes.

Existing options will be made more attractive — more affordable, quicker and easier to use. The Elizabeth Line (Crossrail) will provide a direct rail connection from Heathrow to the West End, the City of London and Canary Wharf for the first time. At Old Oak Common, an interchange to HS2 will transform journey times to the Midlands and the North.

We support the proposed Western Rail Link and Southern Rail Access to Heathrow — schemes that would further enhance the rail network from the west and south. We will also enlarge and strengthen the bus and coach hub at Heathrow, work with operators to increase coach frequencies (priorities including the South West, South Wales and South Coast), and introduce new bus routes serving Maidenhead, Bracknell, Surrey Heath and Elmbridge.

6.2

Colleague travel

We propose to fundamentally change how our colleagues get to, from and around the airport.

We want public transport, walking and cycling to be the default choice for colleague travel — supported by new and improved services, better infrastructure and improved travel information. We will introduce new walking and cycling infrastructure and relocate significant employment sites to locations that can be easily accessed by public transport, on a bike or on foot.

We are introducing new cycle lanes around the airport and expanding our flagship Heathrow Cycle Hub to new locations. We are seeking to create safe cycling routes around the airport campus and on key routes in local communities where colleagues live — Hounslow, West Drayton, Hayes, Feltham, Staines and Slough.

6.3

Car parking

The current amount of Heathrow-related car parking will not increase significantly with expansion but will over time be reallocated from colleague to passenger parking.

Colleague parking will undergo a phased reduction with passenger parking increasing — but with a reduction in the number of parking spaces per passenger from what we have today. Our approach is to consolidate car parking at 'parkways' — areas around the airport with good access to the road network — minimising congestion, reducing the amount of land needed for parking and providing high-frequency links directly to the terminals.

We will increase provision for electric and zero-emissions vehicles, encouraging drivers to use cleaner, less polluting vehicles, and we are committed to reducing parking in residential streets around the airport.

6.4

Road user charging

We propose two charges — a Heathrow Ultra-Low Emission Zone from 2022, replaced by the Heathrow Vehicle Access Charge once the new runway opens.

The Heathrow Ultra-Low Emission Zone (HULEZ) introduces minimum vehicle emissions standards identical to the London Mayor's ULEZ, applying 24/7 to passenger cars and private hire vehicles entering Heathrow's car parks or drop-off areas. It does not apply to local residents or those travelling around the airport without visiting airport facilities. Registered Disabled Passenger Vehicles are exempt.

Following the opening of the third runway (around 2026) we propose to replace HULEZ with the Heathrow Vehicle Access Charge (HVAC). HVAC will apply to all passenger cars, taxis, and private hire vehicles entering the airport's car parks and terminal forecourts regardless of vehicle emission standard. Its main aim is to discourage car trips and establish public transport as the primary means of travel to Heathrow. Blue Badge holders qualify for a 100% discount; private hire and taxi drivers may qualify for a discount based on how often they 'backfill' return trips.

6.5

Taxi and private hire

Taxi and private hire will remain an important mode of travel — providing access to areas with limited public transport options.

We want to ensure taxis and private hire vehicles continue to fulfil this role but with greater efficiency, minimising vehicle trips and benefiting drivers through reducing empty mileage. To do this we want to increase 'backfilling' — matching passengers to drivers so that vehicles are full in both directions, rather than arriving empty to pick up or leaving empty after dropping off. We will also encourage measures to promote taxi sharing, increasing the number of passengers each vehicle carries.

6.6

Freight

Our ambition is to maintain our position as one of Europe's best airports for cargo by improving service and doubling cargo capacity.

Increasing cargo at the airport will not mean doubling the number of HGVs on the road. Our freight proposals are designed to minimise cargo vehicle trips and reduce their effects on local communities while improving reliability and ease of use of cargo operations.

The focus is consolidation — enabling operators to combine cargo consignments onto fewer vehicles. As part of our DCO application we will also provide two areas where drivers can wait off-street before being called up to the Cargo Centre, reducing congestion and HGV parking on local roads.

6.7

Intelligent mobility

Intelligent Mobility uses innovative technologies, ideas and data to improve information and services that help people and goods travel efficiently to, from and around the airport.

We are proposing an Intelligent Mobility Strategy with two pillars: Enhanced Mobility Information Services that improve the timely communication of public transport options to support sustainable travel choices; and a Heathrow Travel Wallet for colleagues that lets us manage, plan and incentivise journeys to Heathrow.

This builds on existing innovations like our Personal Rapid Transit system at Terminal 5 — fully automated vehicles linking parts of the airport — and looks ahead to single-card or smartphone-app access to trains, buses, bikes and cars on demand.
