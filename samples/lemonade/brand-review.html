Tensions

Detected by the brand-review tensions pass

T-typography-split

Hero serif feels editorial; the rest of the page is Lato-bold-commercial

H1 in Merriweather 400 / 55px on a line-art monochrome hero, followed by 8 sections of Lato 700 / 44px. The "editorial moment + commercial body" split has no bridge — and Merriweather itself reads more 2014-era than the brand's other surfaces.

T-rainbow-latent

15-color secondary palette captured in tokens; home renders ~85% monochrome

164 CSS custom properties define a wide rainbow palette (salomie, algae, boston, eucalyptus, cupid, rhino…). On home, only #ff0083 + neutrals + small footer link hovers actually render. The brand's distinguishing palette capability is dormant on its most-trafficked page.

T-cta-allcaps

ALL-CAPS button labels feel dated for a brand whose copy voice is conversational

"Forget everything you know about insurance" is sentence case and warm. "CHECK OUR PRICES" directly below it is uppercase and procedural. The two voices don't share an idiom.

T-section-monotony

Eight content sections of similar visual weight and rhythm

Sections 5–11 alternate white / #f7f7f7 backgrounds with similar height and similar card/illustration patterns. (Almost) 5 Star + Instant everything + Already insured all carry near-identical weight — three social-proof / value-prop sections in sequence with no visual hierarchy.

T-hero-illustration-cropped

The strongest brand illustrations are positioned smallest

Two excellent line-art house illustrations (615 × 471 each) flank the hero copy at the edges, framing it like wallpaper. The bundle-card illustrations are tiny. The pizza diagram — Lemonade's most distinctive how-it-works visual — is buried at section 8. The brand's strongest asset is consistently under-cropped.
