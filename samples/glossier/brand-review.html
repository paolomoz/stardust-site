7 — Tensions

Type scale

No display moment. Hero copy maxes at 20px — only 1.42× over body. The brand voice is doing all the heavy lifting; a refresh could give the hero a typographic moment without changing the family.

Repetition

Two identical product carousels back-to-back. Spring Fever and Fragrance use the same 5-up grid in the same row format, separated only by a thin testimonial band. Visual rhythm is flat.

Colour distribution

Glossier Pink lands once, at the end. The ground colour the brand owns has only one moment on the page. Either commit harder or place a second deliberate punctuation.

Section padding

Flat 32–48px between very different content types. The promo strip, the hero, and the carousels share the same vertical rhythm. The page reads as a single density when it could breathe at the right moments.

Tag overload

Sale-orange stamps across every product card. 30% OFF IN CART, BEST SELLER, NEW, LEAVING SOON, TOP-RATED — five tag types competing for attention in the same grid.

Hero contrast

Overlay text relies on a quiet bottom band of the photo. Borderline AA on darker frames. A deliberate contrast device (scrim, plate, side-cut) would harden the foundation.

8 — Signature moves to preserve

  • Lowercase poetic hero phrasing as a brand voice tell
  • Single-family Apercu typesetting (restraint as identity)
  • Glossier Pink in the footer (don't kill it — earn a second moment)
  • Sharp 0px corners on cards and CTAs
  • Real-person testimonials with overheard moments
  • "Add to bag" verb (not "Buy" / "Shop now")
