A century of stepping people back into a home of their own

One hundred years.Refuge. Resources. Relief. The Road Home opened a desk for travelers in distress at the Salt Lake City Union Pacific Depot in 1923. The desk has not closed since.

Emergency shelter, supportive services, and housing assistance for individuals and families stepping out of homelessness in Salt Lake County. The same work, in the same town, for a hundred years.

What the work has held — for a hundred years and counting.

Three practices, named for the field they belong to. None are new ideas; the patience to keep doing them is the institutional asset.

Emergency Shelter

A safe place to sleep, tonight, every night, for individuals and families.

Supportive Services

Case management, employment, mental and physical health navigation while a person is sheltered.

Housing Assistance

Rapid re-housing, supportive housing, and the patient navigation that holds people in place.
