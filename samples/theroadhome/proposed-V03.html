Refuge. Resources. Relief.
A century of stepping people back into a home of their own.

The Road Home provides emergency shelter, supportive services, and housing assistance that help individuals and families step out of homelessness.

We have done this work in Salt Lake City since 1923, when our staff first opened a desk for travelers in distress at the Union Pacific Depot. Today, on any given night, more than 1,550 people we serve are not in shelter — they are in housing, in the community, where they belong.

Everything we do is to help people overcome homelessness and move back home. That includes the practices the field calls housing-first, progressive engagement, rapid re-housing, and housing navigation — and the stubborn institutional patience to keep doing them, in this town, for a hundred years.

What the year held

In the last reporting year we assisted 3,491 people with employment and housing-related services. Of those who shelter with us, about 60% receive case-management services at some point during their stay. On any given night, 1,550 or more of the people we work with are not in shelter at all — they are in housing.

The numbers describe a method, not a miracle: we keep doing the same things, slowly, in the same place, for the same people, for a hundred years.
