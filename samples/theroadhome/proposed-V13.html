Volume One · Six people · Salt Lake City

Refuge. Resources. Relief.
A photo essay from a hundred-year archive.

Six portraits drawn from The Road Home's housing-stories archive, processed in duotone teal — the colour the agency has used to mark its civic identity for two decades. Captions in the same illustrative-composite spirit the source uses.

The numbers behind the portraits.

In the last reporting year The Road Home assisted 3,491 people with employment and housing-related services. Of those who shelter with us, about 60% receive case-management services at some point during their stay. On any given night, 1,550 or more of the people we work with are not in shelter — they are in housing, in the community, where they belong.

The numbers describe a method, not a miracle: housing-first, progressive engagement, rapid re-housing, and housing navigation. Practiced, in this town, for a hundred years.
