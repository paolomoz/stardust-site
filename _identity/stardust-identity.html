01 · Essence

Design you could only get today.

Stardust is a design-phase toolkit for static-HTML projects. It turns a brand concept and a page brief into curated palettes, grey wireframes, and branded HTML prototypes.

The character

Every design tool promises uniqueness.

Most deliver the average.

Stardust adds a seed — md5(brand · date) — that only exists today, and only belongs to you.

That seed picks from 127 real palettes, names its own clichés so it can subvert them, and produces output you literally could not receive any other way.

Deterministic, not mystical.
Curated, not invented.
Yours alone, today only.

Positioning

For designers and engineers who ship static-HTML sites and don't want their brand to look like every other brand an LLM has ever made.

What makes it different
  • Palette from a curated library127 real palettes from coolors.co/trending, not LLM-invented
  • A divergence toolkitNames the LLM's recurring defaults so it can avoid them
  • Deterministic-random seedsMD5(brand+date) picks decade × craft × register × ground

Clearspace

Clearspace = X on every side.

Reserve at least one core-square unit of empty space around the mark. Don't crowd it with copy, borders, or other marks. The star is a counting device — you can measure the clearspace visually by eyeballing the center cream block.

05 · Typography

Three voices. One hierarchy.

Mono for the seed, display for the name, text for the rest. Mono is not decorative — it marks the places where the tool speaks literally (seeds, commands, provenance). Never use mono for prose.

06 · Voice & tone

Speak like a craftsperson who knows the math.

Stardust is a tool with strong opinions quietly held. It doesn't sell, doesn't mystify, doesn't over-promise. It shows the seed and trusts the reader to get it.

01

Call it the thing.

"Palette," not "chromatic symphony." "MD5 seed," not "cosmic fingerprint." Plain words.

02

Show the seed.

Every choice has a provenance. When something is generated, surface what produced it. Transparency as a feature.

03

Math, not mysticism.

The magic is reproducible. Say "hashed," not "inspired." Say "seeded," not "divined."

04

Honor the designer.

You're the director. Stardust is the crew. Never speak as if the tool made the design.

05

Short is a virtue.

Clipped sentences. No throat-clearing. If a word isn't earning its keep, cut it.
